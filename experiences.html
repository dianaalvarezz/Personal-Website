This will require collecting data from both child and parent. We know that the more a child is exposed to an environment rich with language, the more a child's brain develops. This is because environments rich with language promote more conversational turns, which are non-cry sounds made by the child after being spoken to, which will then lead to more brain development. Conversational turns will be measured through the usage of recorded vest that will be worn by the child throughout their daily activities, which will record language sounds spoken to the child and by the child. The data collected from the recorder vest will then be processed into daily data charts which are ready for analysis. These diaries will be completed once a week for four weeks and will include questions surrounding the amount of time a parent is engaging with their mobile device. Screenshots of mobile device usage data will also be collected through the weekly diary. Through analyzing the correlation between the number of conversational turns between the child and caregiver and the prevalence of mobile device usage, we aim to see understand the relationship between these variables. Child Development

The purpose of this study is to investigate the effectiveness of the LENA Start Program and the -Dekalb Basics Program in promoting high quality and quantity language interactions in children under the age of three. We know that the more a child is exposed to an environment rich with language, the more a child's brain develops. This is because environments rich with language promote more conversational turns, which are non-cry sounds made by the child after being spoken to, which will then lead to more brain development. Both programs are expected to yield a positive increase in conversational turns between the child and the caregiver. Conversational turns will be measured through the usage of recorded vest that will be worn throughout the day by each child as they work through their assigned program, which will record words spoken to the child and by the child. The data collected from the recorder vest will then be processed into daily data charts which are ready for analysis. Through our analysis of the conversational turn done between the child and caregiver before and after the programs, we will be able to tell how effective each program was individually, and which program is more effective overall.
